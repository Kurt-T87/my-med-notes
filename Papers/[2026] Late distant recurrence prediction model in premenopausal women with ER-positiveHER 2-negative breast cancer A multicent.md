# Late distant recurrence prediction model in premenopausal women with ER-positive/HER 2-negative breast cancer: A multicenter retrospective study.

## 🧬 Gemini 临床深度解析

### 1. 分型与人群
*   **分子分型：** HR+/HER2-（雌激素受体阳性/人表皮生长因子受体2阴性）。
*   **疾病阶段：** 早期乳腺癌（术后生存满5年且未复发）。
*   **特定人群：** 绝经前女性（研究入组年龄 ≤45岁）。

### 2. PICO 核心
*   **P (Patient):** 1701名中位年龄41岁、接受过手术且完成至少2年辅助内分泌治疗、术后5年内无复发的绝经前 ER+/HER2- 患者。
*   **I (Intervention):** 延长辅助内分泌治疗（Extended Endocrine Therapy, EET，即总时长 >5年）。
*   **C (Comparison):** 标准时长辅助内分泌治疗（≤5年）。
*   **O (Outcome):** 晚期远处复发（Late DR，指术后5-10年间发生的远处转移）。

### 3. 临床价值
*   **精准分层：** 该研究开发并验证了一个包含8个临床病理变量的机器学习模型，填补了绝经前女性在晚期复发风险评估工具上的空白（区别于常用于绝经后人群的 CTS5 评分）。
*   **优化决策：** 研究结果支持“个体化 EET”。模型定义的**高危患者**能显著从延长治疗中获益（风险降低68%），而**低危患者**获益不明显。这有助于临床医生在保证疗效的同时，减少低危患者因长期内分泌治疗带来的副作用（如骨质疏松、生活质量下降等）。

### 4. 关键数据
*   **预测效能：** 模型曲线下面积 (**AUC**) 达到 **0.78**，表现出较强的区分能力。
*   **复发风险：** 高危组与低危组相比，晚期远处无转移生存期 (DMFS) 的风险比 **HR 为 7.36** (P < 0.001)。
*   **治疗获益：**
    *   **高危组：** 接受 EET 显著改善晚期 DMFS（**HR = 0.32**; 95% CI, 0.18-0.55; P < 0.001）。
    *   **低危组：** 接受 EET 未表现出统计学显著获益（HR = 0.45; P = 0.081）。

### 5. 专家评述
*   **优势：** 针对绝经前这一特殊群体，晚期复发（5年后）是临床难点。此模型利用易获取的临床病理指标实现了较高的预测效能，具有极强的落地性。
*   **偏倚与局限：**
    *   **回顾性设计：** 存在选择偏倚，EET 的决策可能受到当时临床医生经验的影响（例如更重的分期往往更倾向于 EET）。
    *   **治疗方案异质性：** 研究跨度（2000-2011年）较长，期间卵巢功能抑制（OFS）的应用比例和方案可能存在不统一。
*   **后续关注：** 需进一步明确模型中包含的“8个 clinicopathologic variables”具体构成（通常推测包含 T、N、分级、Ki-67 等）。未来可探讨该机器学习模型与多基因检测（如 21-gene RS 或 EndoPredict）的成本效益对比与补充价值。

## 📄 Abstract
Late distant recurrence (DR) remains a significant challenge in estrogen receptor (ER)-positive/Human Epidermal Growth Factor Receptor 2 (HER2)-negative breast cancer, especially in premenopausal patients. This study aimed to develop a machine-learning model predicting late DR risk in premenopausal patients and to assess the clinical benefit of extended endocrine therapy (ET) according to risk stratification.
This retrospective multicenter study included patients aged ≤45 years with ER-positive/HER2-negative breast cancer who underwent surgery between January 2000 and December 2011. This study was designed as a landmark analysis, with the effective baseline set at 5 years after surgery. Eligible patients had five to 10 years of follow-up and received adjuvant ET for at least two years. The primary outcome was late DR, defined as distant metastasis occurring between five and 10 years after surgery.
Among 1701 included patients (median age, 41 years), late DR occurred in 108 patients (6.3%). A machine-learning model using eight clinicopathologic variables demonstrated strong predictive performance (AUC = 0.78). Patients classified as high-risk by the model exhibited significantly worse late DMFS compared to low-risk patients (HR,7.36; 95% CI,4.43-12.20; P<0.001). Among high-risk patients, those who received extended ET had significantly improved late DMFS compared to those who did not (HR,0.32; 95% CI,0.18-0.55; P < 0.001). In low-risk patients, extended ET was not associated with a statistically significant benefit (HR,0.45; 95% CI,0.16-1.22; P = 0.081).
The machine-learning model effectively stratified patients into distinct DR risk groups and highlighted the benefit of extended ET in high-risk patients. This model supports tailored decision-making regarding extended ET in premenopausal patients with ER-positive/HER2-negative breast cancer.
