# Epigenetic and fragment-based profiling across CDK4/6 inhibitors in first-line HR+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer. An ancillary analysis of the MAGNETIC.1 study.
- **Journal**: Breast (Edinburgh, Scotland) (**IF: 7.9**)
- **Published**: 2026-04-01 | **PMID**: 41650543
- **DOI**: [10.1016/j.breast.2026.104703](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.breast.2026.104703)

这篇来自 **MAGNETIC.1** 研究的辅助分析，为我们理解不同 CDK4/6 抑制剂（CDK4/6i）在一线治疗中的生物学差异提供了珍贵的分子视角。以下是针对临床医生的深度解析：

## 🧬 Gemini 临床深度解析

- **分型与人群:** **HR+/HER2- 晚期乳腺癌（MBC）**；接受**一线（1L）**内分泌治疗联合 CDK4/6 抑制剂的患者。

- **PICO 核心:** 
    - **P (Patient):** 149 名接受一线治疗的 HR+/HER2- 晚期乳腺癌患者。
    - **I (Intervention):** 核糖昔利（Ribociclib）或 哌柏西利（Palbociclib）联合内分泌治疗（ET）。
    - **C (Comparison):** 不同 CDK4/6 抑制剂之间的头对头分子动力学对比。
    - **O (Outcome):** 核心指标为无进展生存期（PFS）、总生存期（OS）以及基于 ctDNA 的分子动力学（ESR1 启动子甲基化和 ACTB 片段组学谱）。

- **临床价值:** 
    - **填补证据空白:** 目前尚无哌柏西利、核糖昔利和阿贝西利的随机头对头临床试验。本研究通过液体活检（Liquid Biopsy）在分子层面揭示了药物间的**生物学不等效性**。
    - **耐药预警潜力:** 研究发现不同药物诱导的表观遗传学改变和片段组学动态存在显著差异，这为开发个性化的疗效监测工具和耐药预测模型奠定了基础。
    - **支持 Ribociclib 的优势趋势:** 尽管是探索性分析，但结果在分子水平上印证了 Ribociclib 可能具有更持久的肿瘤抑制效应。

- **关键数据:** 
    - **随访时间:** 中位随访 **34.8 个月**。
    - **生存获益:** Ribociclib 组在 PFS 和 OS 上均呈现**数值上的优势**（虽然在本样本量下未达统计学显著差异）。
    - **分子动力学差异:** 
        - **哌柏西利（Palbociclib）:** 治疗初期出现 **ESR1 启动子甲基化**的瞬时升高，且在 6 个月时出现 **ACTB_short 片段水平的“反弹”**。
        - **核糖昔利（Ribociclib）:** 在相同时间点**未观察到**上述耐药相关的分子动态波动。

- **专家评述:** 
    - **研究局限性:** 该研究属于辅助性、探索性分析，样本量（n=149）有限，不足以得出改变临床指南的结论。
    - **分子深意:** 哌柏西利组观察到的 ACTB 短片段反弹可能暗示了肿瘤克隆的早期进化或隐匿性进展，这解释了为何其在数值上的远期生存略逊于核糖昔利。
    - **后续关注点:** 
        1. **验证必要性:** 需要在更大型的前瞻性队列中验证 ESR1 甲基化和片段化指标作为“早期停药信号”的可行性。
        2. **时间点精细化:** 未来的研究应设置更早（如治疗后 2-4 周）的采样点，捕捉更灵敏的分子早反应（Early Molecular Response）。
        3. **临床决策:** 在面对高肿瘤负荷或具有侵袭性分子特征的患者时，核糖昔利可能在维持分子层面的克隆稳定性上更具优势。

## 📄 Abstract
CDK4/6 inhibitors (CDK4/6i) in combination with endocrine therapy (ET) represent the standard of care for hormone receptor-positive, HER2-negative (HR+/HER2-) metastatic breast cancer (MBC) patients. However, no head-to-head randomized trials have directly compared palbociclib, ribociclib, and abemaciclib. Moreover, predictive biomarkers of resistance to CDK4/6i remain largely undefined. This study aimed to evaluate circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA)-based epigenetic and fragmentomic biomarkers as potential predictors of response and resistance in patients receiving CDK4/6i.
We conducted a biomarker-driven analysis within the prospective, multicenter MAGNETIC.1 study, enrolling 149 patients with HR+/HER2- MBC treated with first-line endocrine therapy and a CDK4/6 inhibitor. Plasma samples were collected at baseline and during treatment (3 and 6 months). Droplet digital PCR was used to assess ESR1 promoter methylation and ACTB fragmentomic profiles. Progression-free survival (PFS) and overall survival (OS) were evaluated, and molecular dynamics were compared between treatment groups.
After a median follow-up of 34.8 months, no statistically significant differences in PFS or OS were observed between ribociclib and palbociclib treated patients, although ribociclib was associated with numerically longer PFS and higher survival rates. At the molecular level, palbociclib treatment was characterized by transient increases in ESR1 promoter methylation at the first evaluation and a rebound in ACTB<sub>short</sub> fragment levels at six months relative to baseline. These dynamic patterns were not observed among patients receiving ribociclib.
ctDNA-based methylation and fragmentomic profiling revealed exploratory, treatment specific molecular dynamics, highlighting biological differences between CDK4/6 inhibitors. These findings support the feasibility of liquid biopsy-based biomarker studies in this setting, although their potential clinical relevance remains preliminary and requires validation in larger cohorts with earlier and more granular on-treatment timepoints.
