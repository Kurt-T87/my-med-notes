# Prognostic Impact of Early Metabolic Response on Interim 18F-FDG PET/CT in HR+/HER2- Metastatic Breast Cancer Treated with CDK4/6 Inhibitors.

## 🧬 Gemini 临床深度解析

- **分型与人群:** 
  本研究聚焦于 **HR+/HER2- 晚期/转移性乳腺癌 (MBC)** 患者。研究对象均为接受 **一线 (1L)** CDK4/6 抑制剂（哌柏西利或瑞波西利）联合内分泌治疗的患者。

- **PICO 核心:** 
    - **P (Patient):** 203 名 HR+/HER2- 晚期乳腺癌患者（2018-2024 年间，两中心回顾性研究）。
    - **I (Intervention):** 治疗早期（2-4 周期后）进行中期 ^18F-FDG PET/CT 检查。
    - **C (Comparison):** 代谢反应者（SUVmax 下降 ≥30%，符合 PERCIST 1.0 标准）与非代谢反应者（SUVmax 下降 <30%）。
    - **O (Outcome):** 主要终点为无进展生存期 (PFS) 和总生存期 (OS)；次要指标包括预测病情的 ROC 分析。

- **临床价值:** 
    本研究强调了 **“早期评价”** 的窗口期价值。在 CDK4/6 抑制剂治疗初期（前 4 个月内），通过 PET/CT 观察到的代谢变化能比传统 RECIST 标准更早识别出潜在的内分泌耐药群体。
    - **识别高危群体:** 对于非代谢反应者，其中位 PFS 仅为 4.8 个月，提示这部分患者存在原发性耐药，临床上可能需要更紧密的随访或考虑提前进入二线方案/临床试验。
    - **指导个体化监测:** 提供了除细胞周期蛋白外的生物影像学动态指标。

- **关键数据:** 
    - **代谢反应率:** 75.4% 的患者实现了早期代谢缓解。
    - **PFS 差异:** 反应者 vs. 非反应者 = **44.4 个月 vs. 4.8 个月** (HR 0.24; p < 0.001)。
    - **OS 差异:** 反应者未达到中位 OS，而非反应者仅为 **32.0 个月** (HR 0.37; p = 0.001)。
    - **耐药特征:** 非反应者基线时呈现更强的侵袭性：肝转移率更高 (34.0% vs. 15.0%)、脑转移率更高 (10.0% vs. 1.3%)、孕激素受体 (PR) 表达更低 (30% vs. 60%)。

- **专家评述:** 
    - **优势:** 该研究展示了极其显著的生存获益梯度（PFS 差异近 10 倍），证明了中期代谢评估在预后分层中的强大效力。
    - **局限与偏倚:** 
        1. **回顾性局限:** 影像采集时间点（2-4 周期）不统一，可能存在时间偏倚。
        2. **预测效能:** 尽管生存曲线差异巨大，但研究者提到 ΔSUVmax 单独预测进展的 ROC 准确度“中等”，这意味着代谢不缓解虽预示预后差，但代谢缓解者中仍有部分患者会迅速进展，需结合分子分型。
    - **后续关注点:** 建议关注 **PR 低表达** 与 CDK4/6i 早期代谢耐药的关联，并在未来前瞻性研究中验证是否可根据 PET 结果进行“治疗降级”或“强化治疗”的临床决策调整。

## 📄 Abstract
<i>Background and objectives</i>: Early biomarkers that can reliably predict treatment outcomes during CDK4/6 inhibitor therapy remain an unmet clinical need in patients with hormone receptor-positive/human epidermal growth factor receptor 2-negative (HR+/HER2-) metastatic breast cancer (MBC). Metabolic changes on ^18F-FDG PET/CT may precede radiologic response and provide insight into tumor biology and early treatment resistance. <i>Methods</i>: This two-center retrospective study included 203 patients with HR+/HER2- MBC who received first-line CDK4/6 inhibitors (ribociclib or palbociclib) plus endocrine therapy between 2018 and 2024. Baseline and interim ^18F-FDG PET/CT scans performed after 2-4 cycles were evaluated. Early metabolic response was defined as a ≥30% reduction in SUVmax on the most metabolically active lesion, consistent with PERCIST 1.0. Progression-free survival (PFS) and overall survival (OS) were analyzed using Kaplan-Meier and multivariable Cox models. ROC analysis assessed the discriminative performance of ΔSUVmax for predicting disease progression. <i>Results</i>: Among 203 patients, 153 (75.4%) achieved a ≥30% SUVmax reduction. Responders had significantly longer PFS (median 44.4 vs. 4.8 months; <i>p</i> < 0.001) and OS (median not reached vs. 32.0 months; <i>p</i> < 0.001). Metabolic response remained independently associated with improved PFS (HR 0.24; 95% CI 0.15-0.37; <i>p</i> < 0.001) and OS (HR 0.37; 95% CI 0.20-0.67; <i>p</i> = 0.001) after adjustment for tumor grade, endocrine resistance, and visceral disease involvement. Non-responders demonstrated more aggressive baseline features, including higher rates of liver (34.0% vs. 15.0%) and brain metastasis (10.0% vs. 1.3%), as well as lower progesterone receptor expression (median 30% vs. 60%). <i>Conclusions</i>: Early metabolic response assessed by SUV-max on interim ^18F-FDG PET/CT is independently associated with substantially improved PFS and OS in HR+/HER2- MBC receiving treatment with CDK4/6 inhibitors. Although the predictive accuracy of ΔSUVmax alone was modest, the strong survival gradient suggests meaningful prognostic value. Prospective studies with standardized imaging time points and comprehensive metabolic metrics are warranted to define the role of PET-guided treatment adaptation.
