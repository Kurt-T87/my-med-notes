# Efficacy and safety of electrochemotherapy in the treatment of cutaneous and sub-cutaneous recurrence from breast cancer: A single-center cohort study.

这篇关于电化学疗法（ECT）治疗乳腺癌皮肤及皮下复发的研究，为临床处理这一棘手并发症提供了重要的局部治疗参考。以下是基于转化医学视角的深度解析：

## 🧬 Gemini 临床深度解析

- **分型与人群:** 
    - **人群：** 经手术、放疗和全身治疗后出现皮肤或皮下复发的乳腺癌患者（通常属于晚期或局部晚期挽救性治疗阶段）。
    - **分型：** 包含多种亚型。研究特别指出 **HER2 阳性** 是预后不良的独立因素。

- **PICO 核心:** 
    - **P (Patient):** 19 名组织学证实的乳腺癌皮肤复发患者（2015-2023 年）。
    - **I (Intervention):** 电化学疗法（ECT）：通过电穿孔技术增加细胞毒性药物（通常为博来霉素或顺铂）进入肿瘤细胞的通透性。
    - **C (Comparison):** 回顾性队列研究，内部对比“缓解者（CR+PR）”与“未缓解者（SD+PD）”。
    - **O (Outcome):** 客观缓解率 (ORR) 为 57.9%；中位总生存期 (OS) 为 19 个月；安全性优异（无 ≥3 级不良反应）。

- **临床价值:** 
    - **局部控瘤利器：** 皮肤复发常伴随疼痛、渗出和感染，严重影响生存质量。该研究证实 ECT 是手术和放疗受限后的有效替代方案。
    - **预后指示：** 研究提示 ECT 的局部反应与 OS 相关，这意味着对局部治疗有反应的患者可能存在更优的系统性生物学背景。
    - **挑战现状：** 尽管目前 ECT 未进入一线指南主推方案，但对于多线耐药后的局部症状管理具有不可替代的价值。

- **关键数据:** 
    - **缓解率：** 57.9% 的患者达到完全或部分缓解。
    - **生存获益：** 缓解组 OS 显著优于未缓解组（20 个月 vs 12 个月，p=0.002）。
    - **风险比：** 治疗无效者的死亡风险是有效者的 **6.59 倍**（HR 6.59）；**HER2 阳性**患者的死亡风险极高（HR 10.67）。

- **专家评述:** 
    - **局限性：** 样本量极小（N=19），且为单中心回顾性研究，存在明显的人群选择偏倚，不能直接推导为 ECT 延长了生存期（可能是由于“能产生缓解”的患者本身生物学行为较温和）。
    - **HER2 悖论：** HER2 阳性患者预后更差，可能反映了该类患者局部侵袭性更强，或者对 ECT 联合的化疗药物不敏感。在抗 HER2 药物丰富的今天，该人群的局部治疗应更强调系统药物与局部手段的协同。
    - **后续关注：** 应关注 ECT 诱导的免疫效应（如“远隔效应” abscopal effect），探讨其是否能通过释放肿瘤抗原增强免疫检查点抑制剂的疗效。

## 📄 Abstract
Cutaneous and subcutaneous recurrence of breast cancer represents a challenging clinical scenario, particularly in patients previously treated with surgery, radiotherapy, and systemic therapies. Electrochemotherapy (ECT) combines electroporation with cytotoxic drug administration and has shown promising local control rates; however, evidence in breast cancer remains limited. This study aimed to evaluate the safety and efficacy of ECT in patients with breast cancer skin recurrence.
We conducted a single-center retrospective cohort study including patients with histologically confirmed breast cancer who developed cutaneous recurrence and were treated with ECT between January 2015 and December 2023. Tumor response was assessed according to RECIST 1.1 criteria. Overall survival (OS) was calculated from the date of the first ECT session to death or last follow-up. Survival analysis compared responders (complete or partial response) and non-responders (no response or progressive disease). Cox regression was used to identify prognostic factors.
Nineteen patients were included. Eleven (57.9%) achieved a complete or partial response. Median OS for the entire cohort was 19 months. Responders had significantly longer OS compared to non-responders (20 vs 12 months; log-rank p = 0.002). Lack of response to ECT was the strongest negative prognostic factor (HR 6.59, 95% CI 1.72-25.29, p = 0.006). HER2 positivity was also associated with poorer survival (HR 10.67, 95% CI 1.32-86.30, p = 0.026). No grade ≥3 adverse events were observed.
ECT appears to be a safe and effective local treatment for cutaneous breast cancer recurrence, providing meaningful local control and a potential survival benefit in responders. Larger prospective studies are warranted to confirm these findings.
