# [Progress of clinical trials on breast cancer in China, 2011-2022].
- **Journal**: Zhonghua zhong liu za zhi [Chinese journal of oncology] (**IF: Unknown**)
- **Published**: 2026-03-23 | **PMID**: 41876202
- **DOI**: [10.3760/cma.j.cn112152-20231129-00340](https://doi.org/10.3760/cma.j.cn112152-20231129-00340)
- **Zotero**: [点击跳转 Zotero 库](zotero://select/items/0_T2CSH9W8)

这篇文献是对中国过去十年（2011-2022年）乳腺癌药物临床试验图谱的宏观审视，反映了从“追随”向“创新”转型的行业背景。作为临床医生，了解这一背景有助于把握未来几年国产药物的市场准入趋势及临床方案的选择。

## 🧬 Gemini 临床深度解析

- **分型与人群:** 全分型覆盖。研究涵盖了针对 **HR+/HER2-、HER2+ 以及三阴性乳腺癌 (TNBC)** 的各类药物；受试人群跨越了**早期（辅助/新辅助治疗）到晚期（解救治疗）**的完整病程阶段。

- **PICO 核心:** 
    - **P (Patient):** 2011年1月至2022年12月期间，在中国国家药监局 (NMPA) 登记平台上注册的乳腺癌临床试验所涉及的所有患者群体。
    - **I (Intervention):** 254种试验性药物（包括156种原研药和174种针对性靶向疗法）。
    - **C (Comparison):** 国内企业与外资企业在试验规模、阶段、药物靶点及作用机制上的差异化对比。
    - **O (Outcome):** 核心指标为临床试验注册数量、靶点分布比例、新药上市获批数量及适应症覆盖范围。

- **临床价值:** 
    1. **研发趋势导向：** 该研究证实了中国乳腺癌研发已进入“靶向时代”，68.5%的试验集中在靶向药物，这与国际主流诊疗趋势高度一致。
    2. **国产替代预期：** 超过75%的试验由国内企业发起，预示着未来几年将有大量高质量、更具成本效益的国产原研药（如CDK4/6i、新型HER2-ADC等）进入临床，有望挑战跨国药企的垄断地位并影响一线方案的经济学决策。
    3. **策略指导：** 揭示了早期乳腺癌（新辅助/辅助）适应症的获批正在增加（占7项），强化了“全程管理”和“治疗前移”的临床理念。

- **关键数据:** 
    - **试验规模：** 401项乳腺癌临床试验，占同期全肿瘤试验的8.0%；2020年达到顶峰（84项）。
    - **靶点高度集中：** **HER2 (28.7%)**、**CDK4/6 (20.1%)** 和 **ER (17.8%)** 是三大核心靶点，三者合计占所有靶向药试验的近70%。
    - **获批成果：** 12年间共有15款新药获批，涵盖19个适应症（12项晚期，7项早期）。

- **专家评述:** 
    1. **靶点扎堆（Me-too现象）：** 数据显示HER2和CDK4/6靶点的竞争异常激烈。虽然增加了临床选择，但也反映出国内创新药研发存在严重的同质化竞争。
    2. **创新力差距：** 尽管数量上占据优势，但在全新靶点（First-in-class）和复杂机制探索上，国内企业与国际巨头仍有代差。
    3. **后续关注点：** 临床医生应密切关注这些“国产创新药”在真实世界研究（RWS）中的表现，特别是针对中国人群特有的药代动力学（PK）数据以及与现有标准方案（SOC）联合应用时的安全性。未来重点将转向耐药机制后的联合用药（如PI3K/AKT/mTOR通路）及免疫检查点抑制剂在特定分型中的应用。

## 📄 Abstract
<b>Objective:</b> To systematically summarize the research progress of clinical trials on breast cancer drugs in China from 2011 to 2022, as well as provide an overview of the marketed drugs. The goal is to offer data and decision-making evidence for relevant departments. <b>Methods:</b> Based on the registration database of the China National Medical Products Administration's Clinical Trial Registration and Information Disclosure Platform, as well as data from the domestic and imported drug query systems, an analysis was conducted on clinical trials of breast cancer drugs, including information on investigational drugs and marketed drugs from January 1, 2011, to December 31, 2022. The study compared differences between Chinese and foreign enterprises in terms of trial scope, trial phases, number of treatment lines, drug types, and mechanisms of action. <b>Results:</b> From 2011 to 2022, a total of 401 clinical trials for breast cancer were registered in China, accounting for 8.0% of the country's overall anti-tumor clinical trials (401/5 011), with 304 trials (75.8%) initiated by domestic enterprises. Over the past decade, clinical trials for breast cancer showed fluctuating growth, reaching a peak of 84 trials in 2020. These trials involved 254 drugs, with 156 (61.4%) being original drugs and 174 (68.5%) being targeted therapies. The most focused targets included human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER-2), cyclin-dependent kinases 4/6 (CDK4/6), and estrogen receptors (ER). There are 50 drugs targeting HER-2 (28.7%), 35 targeting CDK4/6 (20.1%), and 31 targeting ER (17.8%). During the period from 2011 to 2022, a total of 15 drugs for treating breast cancer were approved and launched in China, covering 19 indications. Among these, 7 indications are for adjuvant or neoadjuvant therapy of early-stage breast cancer, and 12 indications are for advanced breast cancer. <b>Conclusions:</b> Substantial achievements have been made in the development of new breast cancer drugs in China from 2011 to 2022. However, there remains a significant gap in the innovation capabilities of domestic pharmaceutical companies compared to international counterparts in the field of breast cancer. Future efforts should be directed towards strengthening research and development in breast cancer, exploring new target points, and investigating combination therapy mechanisms.
